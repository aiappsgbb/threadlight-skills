Why it matters

A demo isn’t a production system.

A green demo doesn’t prove the CISO, SRE and FinOps can sign off. Without that evidence the customer defers — and the pilot dies in the lab.

The stall

“It demos” isn’t “it’s governed.”

No identity, audit trail, eval gate or cost ceiling — nothing a review board can sign. So the pilot waits.

What you get

The scorecard that signs.

13 pillars scored: the posture, every gap with severity and a named owner, and the go-live call — evidence attached.

And it closes them

Every gap names its fix.

Each finding ships the skill that closes it — threadlight or awesome-gbb. Run it, amber turns green. The platform, adopted for you.

What it checks

13 pillars, three architectures, one detected posture.

Thirteen pillars define what “ready” means. Three postures define how it fits your architecture — detected before the first question.

Agent governance — not platform governance

Citadel and the platform team secure the landing zone — the hub, the shared AI gateway, network and policy. Threadlight proves the agent you run in it: these 13 pillars, plus a committed supply-chain SBOM, an agent-identity AI-BOM and an EU AI Act evidence pack. The governance team’s deliverable isn’t a meeting — it’s that record, in the repo, versioned with the code and gating every deploy.

Ship it · the pipeline

Deploy through CI/CD — not your laptop.

One prompt generates the pipeline and the runbooks the platform team runs: federated identity, scoped RBAC, no secret in the repo. Every check becomes a gate — and every gate is a knob you set.

Customize the whole pipeline

One skill, one prompt — these knobs, your call.
--platform
github-actions · azure-devops

GitHub Actions or Azure DevOps — the pipeline is emitted for your CI.

--eval-gatesoft · hard

Warn-only while you tune, or hard-block the merge on a failed quality verdict.

--mcp-gatesoft · hard

Warn-only, or hard-block on an unapproved MCP tool swap against the lockfile.

--target
citadel-spoke · agt · vnet · standard · hybrid

Emit the pipeline for the posture you detected — spoke, in-process, or your own VNet.

--private-network
self-hosted · managed pool

Run every gate on runners inside your network — nothing calls home over the public internet.

Recap

One scorecard — three colours.

The whole engagement reduces to one committed artifact with three honest states — and no fourth.

Green

Evidence found — the pillar is verified, and the proof is committed next to your code.

Amber

A gap with a named owner and a signed waiver — visible, dated, on the clock to green.

Not-verified

Couldn’t observe it — no read access. It never fails the build; it just stays honest.

There’s no red. The scorecard surfaces a posture, not a build failure — so the conversation with the board is about evidence, not blame.
