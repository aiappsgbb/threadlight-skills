Why it matters

A demo isn’t a production system.

A green demo doesn’t prove the CISO, SRE and FinOps can sign off. Without that evidence the customer defers — and the pilot dies in the lab.

The stall

“It demos” isn’t “it’s governed.”

No identity, audit trail, eval gate or cost ceiling — nothing a review board can sign. So the pilot waits.

What you get

The scorecard that signs.

13 pillars scored: the posture, every gap with severity and a named owner, and the go-live call — evidence attached.

And it closes them

Every gap names its fix.

Each finding ships the skill that closes it — threadlight or awesome-gbb. Run it, amber turns green. The platform, adopted for you.

Ship it · the pipeline

Deploy through CI/CD — not your laptop.

threadlight-cicd generates the pipeline and the runbooks the platform team executes: federated identity, scoped RBAC, no secret in the repo.

01 · Two platforms

Your CI of choice.

azd-deploy-prod.yml (GitHub Actions) or azure-pipelines.yml (Azure DevOps), gated on an environment approval.

02 · Secret-free identity

OIDC / Workload Identity Federation.

UAMI + federated credentials. No client secret in the repo — a test asserts it.

03 · Scoped RBAC

Least-privilege, spoke-RG only.

Role assignments scoped to the target resource group — the scorecard’s Identity and RBAC questions, answered in IaC.

04 · Parallel track

Separate from the central platform.

Its own repo — it never touches the shared hub, APIM or platform networking. A spoke consumes the hub through a contract.

Run it on your pilot today

Getting started — four prompts, one scorecard.

You need your pilot in a repo and GitHub Copilot with the Threadlight skills. Azure read-access is optional — without it the cloud pillars resolve to not-verified, nothing fails.

Step 01 · Score the pilot

Point your agent at the skill.

One prompt resolves the posture and walks all thirteen pillars.

> "Use threadlight-production-ready on this pilot. Detect the posture; production load is 800 rpm."
— reads skills/threadlight-production-ready/SKILL.md, resolves posture, walks 13 pillars,
  writes docs/production-readiness-report.md · ready in ~7m

Step 02 · Close the amber gaps

Run the skill that closes each gap.

Each amber pillar has a skill that turns it green — and commits the proof behind it.

> "Use threadlight-govern to scaffold and validate the agent policy artefact."
> "Use threadlight-evals to wire continuous evaluation and an A/B release gate."
> "Use threadlight-redteam to score the AI Red Teaming Agent scan into a verdict."
> "Use threadlight-consumption-iq to forecast the bill at production load."

Step 03 · Gate the pipeline

Generate the secret-free deploy.

An OIDC pipeline with eval, red-team and MCP gates — ready for the platform team.

> "Use threadlight-cicd to generate the OIDC deploy pipeline with eval, red-team and MCP gates."
— writes azd-deploy-prod.yml (or azure-pipelines.yml) + env-setup runbooks · no secret in the repo

Step 04 · Read it & ship

Sign the waivers, hand it over.

Open docs/production-readiness-report.md, sign each amber waiver with its owner, hand the pipeline to the platform team. Everything the board needs is committed — nothing in a console tab to screenshot.
