What a SPEC looks like

Every pilot starts with a SPEC.md.

The library ships one of these per process — the document that comes out of stage one. Same shape every time; the customer’s rules, data, and regulator fill it in. Below is the universal shape, not a specific example.

SPEC.md 12 of 13 sections shown · SpecKit shape · ~5–7 min read

§ 1

Process overview

Customer · domain · target persona · goals · in/out of scope · participants.

§ 2

Process flow

Sequence of agent actions. Decision points, branching, error paths.

§ 3

Business rules

Numbered, testable rules. The audit trail traces every action back here.

§ 4

Data models

Inputs, outputs, intermediate state. Schemas for what flows through.

§ 5

System integrations

Source systems, downstream targets, mock/live swap conventions.

§ 6

Tool contracts

Named tools the agent calls. Input/output JSON shape per tool.

§ 7

Knowledge sources

Grounded retrieval against rules, runbooks, policy, regulator text.

§ 8

Human interaction points

Every consequential step a human approves. Named, signed, audited.

§ 9

Success criteria

Eval scenarios + KPIs. The continuous-eval gate runs against these.

§ 10

Trigger & run model

How the agent runs — chat, schedule, queue, event — with idempotency.

§ 11

Security, compliance & governance

Identity, secrets, posture, residency, regulator obligations.

§ 12

Production readiness

Declared load, 13-pillar scorecard targets, cost forecast inputs.

The chain reads this file, not your prompt. threadlight-design writes it; threadlight-deploy, threadlight-safe-check and threadlight-production-ready all read it.
